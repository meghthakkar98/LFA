To live

or

To Die

It was 3 am, Colin had gotten up to attend nature 's call. After so many sleepless nights, as he counted on his fingers, that was the night he finally had some sleep. He was clean now and finally making amends to the fact that he was not the reason behind his daughter’s death by drowning. Amy’s death was an accident. 4 weeks ago, he wouldn 't have believed that he would be able to move ahead after the divorce. His toxicity was the reason behind it. 'Now i have hope, now I have the will to live.'he reinstated standing in the balcony as he finished his last bit of cigarette. He yearned sleep today.

As he entered the room and shut the lights his phone beeped. “A notification at this time, must be from the operator regarding tomorrow 's due date”, he thought ignoring it. He jumped on his bed, rubbing his eyes ready to sleep again. He was half-asleep half-awake by this time, moving an inch closer to snores with every other breath .The phone beeped again, he woke up with a shriek to buzz it off; for his sound sleep was now his first priority.

Just as he tapped his phone he saw the notification 'KINDLY NOTE, THE NUMBER OF TIMES YOU CAN WAKE UP AGAIN HAS DROPPED TO ZERO’. This was weird. Must be some kind of prank he thought. He dialled the number, and the bot repeated 'KINDLY NOTE, THE NUMBER OF TIMES YOU CAN WAKE UP AGAIN HAS DROPPED TO ZERO’. Ironically his sleep vanished. Colin copied the message and went onto a Google search streak, too damn sure, of it being a prank by some nocturnal who’s too high to sleep.

He received the same voice note as he tapped the search icon. He kept trying, waiting for a sane result to show up but all he saw was 'NO SEARCH RESULTS’. All this while his phone kept buzzing. His inbox was flooded.

The clock struck 6. His eyes had turned red and he reminded himself 'I shouldn 't sleep at any cost 'after cleaning his inbox. He reached his office and was greeted by his colleagues as usual. His phone kept buzzing with the same notification hour after hour.

He crept here and there to see if anybody was watching him over. After his daughter 's death all he did was kept to himself. He had lost contacts with friends and family. Colin had become a loner. As he pondered, a thought struck him 'Maybe the time to meet Amy has come. Maybe this was the way to get to the end.’ His suicidal thoughts were resurging. ‘Suicide by insomnia it is '. It was a reason he made up just to hush his thoughts of the will to live. But there was a part of him who wanted to live, who had moved on. Colin was in a dilemma but he had become too weak to acknowledge it.
‍
As he kept himself awake he completed all deadlines and decided to stop his visits to the therapist. He was determined to not sleep at any cost. As days went by he made it a short routine to recherish memories by revisiting the places he took Amy to. For Colin sleep deprived him of life and life deprived him of sleep. With his insomnia and paranoia his addiction to drugs was recurring back. With the triumph of his hysteria on him, Colin decided to shut himself from everyone completely to the extent that he bought stash of drugs to keep him up on his toes till the day comes. Before this he thought of paying a visit to his ex-wife Daisy and bid her a goodbye. Seeing him in this state she said ‘You started it again didn 't you!! Get out of here!’ and shut the door on his face.

As days went by, he became comfortably numb so much so that he had lost count of the days he had spent in the storeroom with Amy 's things! All Colin could hear was murmurs of some sorts. ‘D..D..D..ad’. As he snort the last bit of his pack he fell on the bed with a thud. His snores turned into silence as he slept the last time in solace.
It was time to WAKE UP.!


- Hitesh Bhatia, Sem-7, Chemical

Fomo

The world is an ocean ofopportunities. It serves you variety of them in each and every areaof activity. And social media is icing on the cake. It helps peopleto share their thoughts, their activities and their achievements. Inthis hyper connected society, people can easily get to know about allthe opportunities, other people are getting and the way those peopleexhaust all the possibilities out of it.
With these opportunitiescomes the temptation to do them all. Our lives are influenced greatlyby what we see and hear. These influences give rise to ourtemptation, which in turn gives rise to FOMO-Fear of Missing Out. Ahuman being desires to fulfill his/her temptations. Watching peopleachieving their dreams and completing their bucket lists can causeone to feel obliged to fulfill his own temptations.
Our life nowadays mainlydepends on social media. Our mood changes according to the feed ofother people. Nowadays everyone is so much indulged in living outtheir lives inspired by the influencers, celebs or movies that somepeople hate to admit that they want to avoid something which istrending, even if they want to. Similarly, we are easily attracted towhat others do and feel bound to involve ourselves in the same.We are mesmerized by theways other people present their life on social media. This gives us afeeling of being disappointed by our own selves, considering this asa fact that we are not able to cope up with the present day norms. Aperson is also influenced by peers. Because of the peer pressure, aperson tries to fit himself/herself into an environment, no matter ifthey belong there or not. It is like a puzzle piece belonging to onejigsaw trying to fit into another. Among these things, there existpeople who find bliss by being passed out, and ruffling their fingersthrough pages of a good book while other people are traipsing fromvenue to venue searching for a place to hold a beer in hand. There are people whoinstead of meeting a new bunch of people every day, find comfort inthe same small group of people where everyone can portray their trueselves. These people can be considered to be living a lifestyleinfluenced by JOMO- Joy of Missing Out. The Joy of Missing Out has anability to liberate one’s soul from the overstimulated societaldrama, by inspiring people to live in the moment for themselves, notfor the others. It encourages us to understand what we want, why wewant it, and how we’re feeling in the present moment. It encouragespeople to opt OUT and say NO. JOMO gives a person’ssoul a chance to live and enjoy the present peacefully. It directsyou towards your actual liking. It is like a person dating theirsoul. It helps you slow down the rushing life. You can live your lifeat your own pace. You can actually live in the moment and as said byNaina (from YJHD), you can’t be everywhere, no matter how much youtry, there is something you will always miss out, so why not enjoy inthe present. Bejoyful. Be simple. Be together. Be real. Be messy. Make lifepersonal. Remove the arms-length distractions of technology. Choosereal connections rather than shallow distractions. Each moment wehave is a gift. Will you choose fear or joy?
-Foram Shah,SEM-5,EC.

Midst the Chaos

Entrepreneur

or

Wantrepreneur ?

There is a very famous quote from one of the web series  "Jab is desh k graduates apni 9-5 job se bore hone lagte hain tab bahar nikalne k lie unhe 3 raste dikhte he MBA, IAS or STARTUP ".

Well true it is. Most of the people during their college life think about an idea and about how life-changing that idea is but most of them also give up on their ideas, as the path to becoming an entrepreneur is not that easy. An entrepreneur is a person who converts an idea into a real business, and wantrepreneur is the one whose ideas remain as just ideas.

We all must have felt that feeling of excitement whenever we watch TVF 's Pitchers or the very famous Silicon Valley. From that, we all must have also felt those sparks fading away, and the main reason for that is the question which we ask to ourselves "Is my idea one in a million?".

90%of the startups fail, as you are reading the article, probably 40 new startups were created across the world and 85 already existing businesses shutdown!  But it is also a fact that ever since Mark Zuckerberg hit the jackpot with Facebook, many young entrepreneurs have come up with great innovations and successful startup businesses.

Starting up a startup is not achieved without stumbling with a few rocks on the road, but as truly as the saying goes that it all starts with just an idea. The best ideas normally emanate from your own experiences and your immediate surroundings. Look at your idea on a long term basis, will your idea be able to run in the long term. If so, then you are on the right path.

The second most important thing when building a startup is seed funding, and we all have an advantage here as colleges provide numerous opportunities to get funds for our dream ideas. It is required to analyze the startup and expenditure well. Initially, students can aim for receiving funding from incubation and acceleration centres of their college. Students can also participate in various competitions like Hackathons and Ideathons like the Smart India Hackathon, SSIP Gujarat state Hackathon and various college-level events. They can also present their business ideas at BPlan competitions like Eureka (IIT Bombay) or Empresario(IIT KGP) etc. As soonas the product is launched in the market, they can pitch for higher-level funding.

Apart from idea and funding, it is important to secure your intellectual property in which basically, a company has its own right to create a product. Examples include patents, copyrights etc which gives the company a differentiating factor.

Branding and social media marketing are also equally important as it is required so that your product has an impact on people 's minds.

One of the best ways to set your startup is to write up a business plan based on your market research. Building a website or an app for your product is the new way to enter up into the startup market.

While the entrepreneur may have big dreams, it is ultimately the team whose support he/she requires to reach the goals. Hence, hiring the right team is an important and crucial step for the startup.

At the end what 's most important is never losing that spark for your business because you never lose in business. Either you win or you learn.
- Vritika Malhotra,Sem 7,IT

About

Us

LFA LJITES FETIALIS AEVUM is LJIET’s biannual magazine. It is edited, designed, marketed and photographed by LJITES, the students of LJIET. It started in the year 2012, 8 years ago with issue #1. At first, it was just an idea by a group of students but its an integral part of LJIET. With 16 issues launched, LFA is now a structured team divided into three main modules and two sub-modules. The three main modules which work hard each semester for the launch of the issue are Editing Department, Marketing Department and Designing Department. The other two Sub-modules Social Media Team and Photography Team help these three main modules in the creation of new editions of LFA.

LFA team handles everything like writing, proofreading, designing pages, getting sponsorships, keeping a budget, launching the magazine, distributing the magazines and much more. Students of the college part of LFA team handle everything under the guidance of LFA 's Faculty Co-ordinator , Prof. Parth Sinroza, who is associated with LFA since the beginning.
